Climate change is increasingly impacting every aspect of our lives, from food security to health and the global economy. Rising temperatures lead to extreme weather events like droughts, floods, and storms, which cause crop failures and displace communities. This is especially unfair for developing nations, which contribute the least to emissions but face the harshest consequences. As young people, we're beginning to push for climate justice, calling on governments and corporations to take responsibility by investing in green technology and reducing waste. While individual actions like reducing plastic use help, real change requires systemic shifts that address the root causes of emissions and ensure a sustainable future for all. Junk food may be tasty, but its side effects on health are concerning. High in sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats, junk food can lead to weight gain, high blood pressure, and increased cholesterol, which raise the risk of heart disease. Regularly consuming junk food also impacts metabolism and blood sugar levels, which can lead to diabetes. Additionally, it often lacks essential nutrients, leading to deficiencies that affect energy levels and mental health. Over time, a diet heavy in junk food can seriously harm both physical and mental well-being.
